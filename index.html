Anish Sarma

I'm a control theorist, electrical engineer, and wet-lab biologist. My research aims to understand how multicellular organisms repair themselves, and to use this understanding to address challenges in medicine and climate. This research raises fundamental and complementary questions in biology and control theory. Within biology and bioengineering, I work mostly in immunology and developmental-regenerative biology, though I've also had a long and happy association with neuroscience and neural engineering. Within control theory and electrical engineering, I develop mathematical foundations for the design of localized, self-repairing, and sustainable networked systems.

I'm presently writing my Ph.D. dissertation at Caltech, advised by John Doyle and informally co-advised by Lea Goentoro; previously, I was a staff research engineer with the BrainGate clinical trial.
