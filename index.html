Anish Sarma

I'm a control theorist, electrical engineer, and wet-lab biologist working at the interface of living and engineered systems. My research aims to understand how multicellular organisms repair themselves; to develop mathematical foundations in control theory for localized, self-repairing, and sustainable systems; and to build technologies to address related challenges in medicine and climate. While much of my applied work is in immunology and developmental-regenerative biology, I've also had a long and happy association with neuroscience and neural engineering in relation to these themes.

I'm presently writing my Ph.D. dissertation at Caltech, advised by John Doyle and informally co-advised by Lea Goentoro; previously, I was a staff research engineer with the BrainGate clinical trial.
