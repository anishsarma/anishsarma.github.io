What is control theory?

Control theory is the formal mathematical study of systems that respond to uncertain and dynamic environments, which we call control systems. Likewise, control engineering is the practice of designing control systems. In biology and medicine (as well as neuroscience and ecology), we might also think of control science, the practice of designing experiments and making inferences about natural or extant control systems.

Often a mathematical theory of control systems in general guides the selection of particular mathematical models, which guides the selection of experiments, which guides the design of devices and therapies. A control framework therefore spans theoretical, basic science, translational, applied, and clinical approaches.
